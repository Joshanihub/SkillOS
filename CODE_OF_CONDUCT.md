# SkillOS Community Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

SkillOS is dedicated to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free community for all members, regardless of:
- Age
- Body size
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We are committed to creating an environment where everyone can contribute meaningfully and feel valued.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors We Encourage

- **Respectful Communication:** Treat all members with dignity and respect
- **Inclusive Language:** Use language that welcomes people of all backgrounds
- **Constructive Feedback:** Provide thoughtful, actionable feedback on ideas and contributions
- **Accountability:** Take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them
- **Collaboration:** Work together to solve problems and improve the project
- **Patience:** Understand that people are learning and may need time

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in the SkillOS community:

- **Harassment:** Unwelcome behavior based on protected characteristics
- **Discrimination:** Treating people unfairly based on identity or background
- **Trolling:** Deliberately posting inflammatory content to provoke reactions
- **Personal Attacks:** Insulting, demeaning, or attacking individuals
- **Threats:** Any form of threat or intimidation
- **Sexual Harassment:** Unwanted sexual comments, advances, or behavior
- **Spam:** Repeatedly posting off-topic content or promotional material
- **Doxxing:** Sharing personal information without consent
- **Bad Faith Engagement:** Participating with the intent to disrupt or harm

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All SkillOS project spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- SkillOS events and community gatherings
- Community members representing SkillOS in public spaces
- Unofficial spaces created by community members for SkillOS discussion

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Document the behavior:** Note what happened, when, where, and who was involved
2. **Report it:** Contact the project maintainers at [project-email@example.com] or via a private message to a maintainer
3. **Be specific:** Include links, timestamps, and direct quotes when possible
4. **Stay safe:** Do not confront the person if you feel unsafe

Reports are handled with confidentiality. We do not name reporters publicly without their permission.

### Response Process

Upon receiving a report:

1. The maintainers will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. An investigation will begin (typically within 1 week)
3. The reporter and reported person may be asked for additional information
4. A decision will be made based on the evidence
5. Actions will be taken according to the severity of the violation

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

- **First Offense:** Warning and request to correct behavior
- **Repeated Violations:** Temporary suspension from community spaces
- **Severe Violations:** Permanent ban from the project and community

The project maintainers have the right to temporarily or permanently ban anyone who engages in severe violations.

## Community Guidelines

### In Issues and Pull Requests

- Read existing discussions before posting
- Follow the project's templates and guidelines
- Be respectful when disagreeing with maintainers or contributors
- Provide context and examples with your contributions
- Acknowledge the work of others and give credit appropriately

### In Discussions

- Stay on topic
- Search before asking — your question may already be answered
- Be patient with new community members
- Share knowledge generously
- Celebrate wins and progress together

### In Code Reviews

- Review with the goal of improving code quality, not criticizing people
- Ask questions to understand before critiquing
- Suggest improvements, don't demand them
- Acknowledge good work
- Be open to feedback on your own contributions
