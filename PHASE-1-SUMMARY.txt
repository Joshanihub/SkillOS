-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
KEY DESIGN DECISIONS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. 13 Required Components (not flexible)
   WHY: Forces completeness. Incomplete systems are unreliable.

2. 10-Phase Skill Creator (not skippable)
   WHY: Each phase contributes to reliability. Compressed process produces weaker skills.

3. Three Validation Tests (minimum, not maximum)
   WHY: Tests catch critical failure modes. More tests welcome, three are baseline.

4. No Defaults (language/region/infrastructure)
   WHY: Privilege breaks equity. World-first means truly world-first.

5. Global Regulatory Awareness
   WHY: GDPR, NDPR, Kenya DPA, PDPA, LGPD, CCPA, HIPAA all equally valid.
   No region gets to claim its laws are default.

6. Ethics Before Output Generation
   WHY: After-the-fact ethics doesn't catch problems. Infrastructure prevents them.

7. MIT License (non-negotiable)
   WHY: Owned by the world means everyone can modify, deploy, fork freely.
